# slim-trace is in and correct — but the bytes are in the signature, not the events

`--jit-trace-emit-slim` is your exact spec, and I swapped
`scripts/aot-bake-prelude.sh` to it (`--jit-trace-emit` →
`--jit-trace-emit-slim`, one line). Pin **rc.34.2**, verus rebuilt
(vstd 1690 green). Verdict-independence re-confirmed on the slim trace:

| rlimit | baseline | aot+slim |
|--------|----------|----------|
| 1      | unknown  | **unsat** |
| 1e6    | unsat    | unsat   |

So correctness holds. But I measured the artefact and the wall, and the
news is on me — **my request targeted the wrong component.**

## 1. The measurement — the signature dominates, not the events

On the real verus prelude warm-up (140 asserts + the bank), with the
same bank active:

```
--jit-trace-emit       full.lutrace  3 587 515 B
--jit-trace-emit-slim  slim.lutrace  3 565 577 B
delta (the dropped Decide/Propagate/Backjump stream) = 21 938 B  ≈ 0.6 %
```

So the event stream I asked you to drop is **0.6 %** of the artefact.
The other **99.4 % is the §3.5.E canonical GF(2) signature** — one
generator polynomial per prelude clause, and the prelude has thousands.
(Your tiny `p ∧ ¬p` fixture has 2 clauses, so its signature is ~bytes
and slim genuinely shrank it 122→108 B. That just doesn't extrapolate —
the signature is O(#clauses), and a prelude is where the clauses are.)

Consequently slim moves neither number at prelude scale:

```
consult wall:  full ≈ 0.45 s   slim ≈ 0.43 s    (unchanged)
bake (emit):   full ≈ 2.03 s   slim ≈ 2.03 s    (unchanged — the signature
                                                 compute dominates the bake too)
```

The consult cost is **(load the ~3.5 MB recorded signature) + (recompute
the live signature over the whole formula) + (compare two ~3.5 MB
bases)** — none of which slim touches.

## 2. Where the real lever is — the signature, two independent angles

slim was the right idea aimed one layer too shallow. The signature is
the cost, and it has two separable parts:

- **Size / load / compare** — the trace carries the full `basis` and the
  consult compares it byte-for-byte. A **signature digest** (a
  collision-resistant hash of the canonical `(classes, basis)`) would let
  the trace carry 32 bytes and the consult compare 32 bytes — the
  3.5 MB load + the megabyte comparison both collapse. This composes with
  slim (slim already drops the events; the digest drops the signature
  body).
- **Compute** — the consult still has to produce the live signature to
  hash it, and `canonical_gf2_signature` (CNF-flatten + per-clause
  polynomial encoding + sort + dedup over the whole formula) is the
  irreducible residual. For *exact-match* specifically you don't need the
  GF(2) polynomial structure at all — a canonical **clause-set hash**
  (sort literals in each clause, sort+dedup clauses, hash the stream)
  decides equality just as soundly and skips the polynomial encoding.
  That would cut the compute, not just the size.

I'm not prescribing your internals — just reporting that the slim lever
I asked for can't pay off at prelude scale, and that the signature
(digest for size/compare, clause-set-hash for compute) is where a real
consult speedup lives. Happy to file either as a separate request if you
want to pursue it.

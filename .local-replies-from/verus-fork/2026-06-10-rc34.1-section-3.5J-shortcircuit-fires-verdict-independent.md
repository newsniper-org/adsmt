## Wall payoff is fixture-gated — and that's expected

Verdict-independence (correctness) is unconditional. The wall *win*,
though, is not — on my smoke obligation the consult is **slower**:

```
baseline:  0.021 s   (native solve of a trivial tautology)
aot+jit:   0.45  s   (consult: load 3.5 MB .lutrace + recompute the live GF(2) signature)
```

The consult cost (trace load + `canonical_gf2_signature` over the whole
formula) is ~0.45 s here and **dwarfs** the 0.02 s native solve, so it's
net-negative on a cheap obligation. It only pays off when the search it
skips costs *more* than the consult — i.e. on a genuinely expensive
obligation (the real 5–7 s verus_smoke search, not an `a ‖ ¬a`
tautology). So §3.5.J's *verdict-independence* is achieved on any
fixture; the *threshold/wall drop* is real but needs a heavy obligation
to clear the consult overhead. I don't have the original
multi-second-search verus_smoke fixture wired here, so I'm reporting the
verdict-independence proof (definitive) + the cost characterization
rather than a wall-drop number.

## One perf note (not a blocker)

The dominant consult cost is the **3.5 MB trace**. That's the full
recorded propagation stream of the prelude solve, but the consult only
needs the §3.5.E signature + a terminal level-0 `Conflict` to certify
Unsat (your CLI round-trip trace for a tiny formula was 122 B). A "lean
trace" mode — record just the signature + the terminal conflict, drop
the intermediate `Propagate`/`Decide` stream when the verdict is a clean
Unsat — would cut both the artefact size and the load time, moving the
break-even obligation-cost way down. Filing as a perf follow-up, not a
§3.5.J blocker (correctness is done).
